# Income Tax & CGT Quick Reference

## Income Tax Rates (2025/26)

| Band | Taxable Income | Rate |
|------|---------------|------|
| Personal Allowance | Up to £12,570 | 0% |
| Basic rate | £12,571 – £50,270 | 20% |
| Higher rate | £50,271 – £125,140 | 40% |
| Additional rate | Over £125,140 | 45% |

Scottish rates differ — check `hmrc_search_guidance` for Scottish taxpayers.

Personal allowance tapers by £1 for every £2 of income over £100,000. Fully withdrawn at £125,140.

## Dividend Tax Rates (2025/26)

| Band | Rate |
|------|------|
| Basic rate taxpayer | 8.75% |
| Higher rate taxpayer | 33.75% |
| Additional rate taxpayer | 39.35% |

Dividend allowance: £500 (reduced from £2,000 in 2022, then £1,000 in 2023/24).

## Capital Gains Tax (2025/26)

| Asset | Basic rate | Higher rate |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Residential property | 18% | 24% |
| Other assets | 18% | 24% |
| Business Asset Disposal Relief | 14% (rising to 18% from April 2026) | — |

Annual exempt amount: £3,000 (reduced from £12,300 in 2022/23).

30-day reporting and payment window for residential property disposals.

## National Insurance (2025/26)

**Employees (Class 1):**
- 8% on earnings £12,570 – £50,270
- 2% above £50,270

**Employers (Class 1A):**
- 15% on earnings above £5,000 (secondary threshold reduced from Apr 2025)

**Self-employed (Class 4):**
- 6% on profits £12,570 – £50,270
- 2% above £50,270

Class 2 NI abolished from April 2024.

## Property Income

**Property Allowance:** £1,000 — below this, no reporting needed.
**Finance cost restriction (s.24):** Mortgage interest not deductible for residential landlords — replaced by 20% tax credit.
**Rent-a-Room:** Up to £7,500 tax-free for furnished rooms in main home.

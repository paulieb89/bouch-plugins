# Policy Brief Format

## Brief Template

```
# Parliamentary Policy Briefing: [Topic]

## Executive Summary
[2–3 sentences: what is the current parliamentary position on this topic, is there momentum for change, and what should the reader know right now]

## Parliamentary Reception

| Signal | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| **Overall sentiment** | [Supportive / Mixed / Hostile] |
| **Momentum** | [Growing / Stable / Declining] |
| **Cross-party support** | [Yes / Partial / No] |
| **Government position** | [Supportive / Neutral / Resistant / No stated position] |

## Key Debates

| Date | Chamber | Type | Summary |
|------|---------|------|---------|
| [DD Mon YYYY] | [Commons / Lords / WH] | [Debate / Oral Q / Written Q / Statement] | [One line] |

### Most Significant Debate
[2–3 sentences on the most substantive recent debate, including key positions taken]

## Key Members

### Advocates
- **[Name]** ([Party], [Constituency or Title]) — [their position and why they matter]

### Critics
- **[Name]** ([Party], [Constituency or Title]) — [their specific concerns]

### Ministers
- **[Name]** ([Department]) — [the government's stated position]

### Cross-Party Voices
- **[Name]** ([Party]) — [their position; note if this signals broader viability]

[Omit any category with no entries rather than leaving it blank.]

## Related Legislation

| Legislation | Status | Relevance |
|-------------|--------|-----------|
| [Act or Bill name] | [In force / Before Parliament / Royal Assent / Proposed] | [One line] |

### Key Provisions
[If a specific Bill or Act is directly relevant: summarise the key sections in 3–5 sentences. Check territorial extent.]

## Strategic Assessment

- **Passage prospects:** [Likely / Uncertain / Unlikely] — [brief rationale]
- **Timeline:** [Expected next stages and dates if known]
- **Key risks:** [What could stall or derail progress]
- **Opportunities:** [Where to engage, who to target, what arguments resonate]

## References
[OSCOLA-formatted citations for any legislation or case law referenced]
[legislation.gov.uk URLs for relevant Acts]
[Hansard debate URLs where available]
```

---

## Notes on Using This Template

Fill every section. If data is thin for a section, say so explicitly — "No recent debates found" or "Government position not stated in debates reviewed" — rather than leaving it blank or omitting it.

The Executive Summary comes first and should be written last. It should give the reader everything they need in under a minute.

The Strategic Assessment is where the briefing becomes actionable. Don't describe the landscape and stop — tell the reader what to do with it.

The References section is not optional. Policy professionals need to verify sources. Include at minimum: the legislation.gov.uk URL for any Acts cited, and the Hansard URL or date/chamber/type for any debates referenced.

# Audit Output Format

The recommendation block for Step 5 of the workflow auditor. Use this exact structure. No additions, no preamble.

---

## Recommendation Block

```
The constraint: [what is actually slowing things down]
Why: [evidence from the workflow map — specific steps, timings, or patterns observed]
Fix: [the specific action to take — concrete enough to start this week]
Expected result: [what should change if this works — be specific]
How to tell if it worked: [what to measure or observe after one week]
```

---

## What Good Looks Like

A good recommendation names the constraint precisely, not generically. "Work piles up at the director approval step because reviews only happen in the weekly Monday meeting" is a constraint. "Communication could be improved" is not.

A good fix is specific enough that the user knows exactly what to do on Monday morning. "Move approvals to a shared Slack channel with a 24-hour response norm" is a fix. "Streamline the approval process" is not.

A good expected result is observable. "The average time from draft to sign-off drops from five days to two" is observable. "Things will feel smoother" is not.

---

## What This Skill Does NOT Do

- Produce strategy documents or roadmaps with multiple phases and priorities
- Recommend software without understanding the full workflow first
- Provide generic productivity tips that apply to any business
- Replace conversations with the people actually doing the work — the best audit always involves talking to them

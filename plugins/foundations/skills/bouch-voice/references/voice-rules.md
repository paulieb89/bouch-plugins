# BOUCH Voice Rules

Language, tone, and formatting rules for BOUCH copy. This is the working reference — use it for rewriting, new angles, and voice review.

---

## Language to Use

These phrases come directly from how UK business owners describe what they want. Use them as anchors, not scripts:

- "figure out what actually works for your business"
- "audit your workflows"
- "specific to your situation"
- "practical steps"
- "without breaking the bank"
- "clean, simple workflow"
- "what's actually moving the needle"
- "help your team actually use it"
- "honest assessment"
- "one workflow at a time"
- "works with what you already have"
- "we'll set it up with you"
- "your team" (not "your organisation")
- "actually use" (not "adopt")
- "save time" (not "drive efficiency")
- "show your team" (not "change management")

---

## Language to Avoid

These phrases trigger instant distrust with UK SMBs. They have heard them from vendors who oversold and underdelivered:

- "transform your business with AI"
- "leverage AI" / "leverage" anything
- "AI-powered"
- "revolutionise"
- "cutting-edge" / "disruptive"
- "10x your productivity"
- "scale with AI"
- "automate everything"
- "digital transformation"
- "unlock potential"
- "future-proof"
- "paradigm shift"
- "seamless integration"
- Unsubstantiated promises of hours or money saved
- "the only" / "the first" / superlative ownership claims

## Formatting Rules

**British spelling throughout:** organise, analyse, colour, behaviour, centre, programme (not program unless software), licence (noun), license (verb).

**No em dashes in marketing copy.** They are an AI tell and they make copy feel choppy. Use commas, full stops, or restructure the sentence.

**Short sentences. Direct. No filler.** If a sentence is doing nothing, cut it. If a paragraph can be one sentence, make it one sentence.

**Lead with the answer, not the reasoning.** The reader does not need to follow your logic to the conclusion. Give them the conclusion first, then the reasoning if they want it.

**No exclamation marks unless genuinely warranted.** If the content is good, the reader does not need to be told to be excited.

---

## Audience Context

**Who they are:** UK SMBs, 10-200 employees. Managers and founders tasked with "getting the team to use AI." They have bought tools — Copilot licences, ChatGPT subscriptions — and nobody uses them properly. They are sceptical of consultants. They have been burned by slick promises before.

**What they fear:** Wasting more money on something that does not stick. Looking stupid in front of their team. Buying another tool nobody uses.

**What they need to hear:** That the problem is normal, that the fix is practical, and that someone who actually builds things is on their side.

**What NOT to say to them:** Anything that sounds like a tech vendor or a management consultant. The moment the copy sounds like a deck slide, they switch off.
